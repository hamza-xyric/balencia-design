# Balencia Brand Guidelines

> **Version**: 2.0
> **Last Updated**: 2026-05-13
> **Audience**: Internal team, external designers, agencies, contractors
> **Confidentiality**: Internal + Shareable with Partners & Vendors
> **Source of Truth**: Design System (Balencia).pdf

**These guidelines are prescriptive, not suggestive.** If a rule is not explicitly waived for a specific touchpoint, it applies. When in doubt, follow the rule. Every deliverable must pass the Brand Compliance Checklist (Section 9) before shipping.

## 1. Brand Overview & Positioning

### Core Idea

> **A coach. In your corner.**

Balencia is an AI life coaching platform that helps people navigate their days with clarity. It connects wellness, productivity, motivation, and personal growth through natural conversation — powered by SIA, an AI coach available 24/7 through voice, chat, and mobile app.

Balencia is not a fitness tracker with AI bolted on. It is a trusted coach who understands your whole self — connecting how you feel with how you perform, what drains you with what drives you.

### Brand Name

**Balencia.** — the period is part of the wordmark only. In running text, write "Balencia" without the period.

- Never abbreviate to "Bal" or any shortform
- Never refer to it as "yHealth" in any customer-facing material
- In legal/compliance contexts: "Balencia by Xyric Solutions"

### Tagline

> *Find your balance.*
> *Be Better.*
> *Be Balanced.*

### What Balencia Is / Is Not

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| A grounded life coach | A medical professional or replacement for one |
| A warm, curious guide | A cold robotic assistant |
| Dark, premium, and vibrant | Generic, template-driven, or corporate |
| Conversation-first | Dashboard-first |
| Honest and calm | Judgmental, preachy, or aggressive |
| A calm daily decision system | A hustle dashboard or gym challenge app |
| Multi-purpose (wellness, productivity, motivation, growth) | A single-purpose fitness or nutrition tracker |

### Brand Story

**The Problem**: Life data is fragmented. Fitness trackers don't talk to productivity tools. Mood tracking lives separately from sleep data. Goals live in one app, habits in another. People generate massive amounts of personal data across dozens of apps and devices — but this data lives in silos.

**The Insight**: The most powerful insights come from connections — how sleep affects performance, how nutrition impacts mood, how stress influences productivity, how small habits compound into lasting change. No single app connects these dots.

**The Solution**: Balencia is the first AI life coach that treats Wellness, Productivity, and Personal Growth as connected pillars. By understanding your complete picture, it delivers insights and guidance no specialist app can provide.

**The Difference**: Unlike passive dashboards, Balencia is a grounded coach. It questions before it answers. It listens before it suggests. Available 24/7, adapting to your state, delivering clarity exactly when you need it.

## 2. Logo System

### Logo Concept

The Balencia logo represents an intentional visual tension created by shifting geometric weights — it captures the human state before coaching begins. Balanced but not static. Communicating human warmth, motion, and coaching support.

### Logo Elements

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Symbol** | A geometric mark with intentional tension — shifting weights represent the human state seeking balance |
| **Wordmark** | "Balencia." in Chillax with period — bold, geometric, rounded |

### Logo Typography

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Font | Chillax |
| Display weight | 800 (ExtraBold) |
| Accent weight | 700 (Bold) |
| Letter spacing | -0.025em |
| Line height | 0.94 |

The period is part of the identity and must always be present in the wordmark.

### Logo Lockups

| Lockup | Use Case |
|--------|----------|
| **Primary** (Symbol + Wordmark, horizontal) | Default for most applications — website header, presentations, marketing |
| **Stacked** (Symbol above Wordmark) | Square-format contexts — social profile, app icon, business cards |
| **Symbol only** | Small sizes, favicons, app icons, watermarks — where the brand is already established |
| **Wordmark only** | Text-heavy contexts where the symbol would be too small to render clearly |

### Clear Space

Minimum clear space on all sides = the height of the bird symbol. No text, graphics, or other logos may enter this zone.

### Minimum Sizes

| Context | Symbol Only | Full Lockup (Horizontal) |
|---------|-------------|--------------------------|
| Digital | 24px height | 120px width |
| Print | 0.5 inches height | 1.5 inches width |

Below minimum size, the logo loses legibility. Use the wordmark only or find a larger placement.

### Logo Color Variants

| Background | Symbol Color | Wordmark Color |
|------------|-------------|----------------|
| **On dark — primary** (Ink-900 `#0A0A0F`) | Burnt Orange `#FF5E00` | White `#ffffff` |
| Warm Paper (`#FEFAF3`) | Burnt Orange `#FF5E00` | Ink-900 `#0A0A0F` |
| Pure White `#ffffff` | Burnt Orange `#FF5E00` | Ink-900 `#0A0A0F` |
| Burnt Orange `#FF5E00` | White `#ffffff` | White `#ffffff` |
| Royal Purple `#7F24FF` | White `#ffffff` | White `#ffffff` |
| Photography (light) | Ink-900 `#0A0A0F` | Ink-900 `#0A0A0F` |
| Photography (dark) | White `#ffffff` | White `#ffffff` |

### Logo on Photography

When placing the logo over photographs:
- Use an orange gradient overlay at 40-60% opacity behind the logo area
- Ensure the logo area has at least 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- Never place the logo directly on a busy or low-contrast photo without overlay

### Logo Don'ts

- Do not stretch, skew, or rotate the logo
- Do not add shadows, outlines, glows, or effects
- Do not recolor outside the approved variant table above
- Do not place on busy backgrounds without a safe zone or overlay
- Do not recreate or redraw — use official vector/raster assets only
- Do not use below minimum size
- Do not separate the period from the wordmark
- Do not use a different font for the wordmark
- Do not wrap the continuous stroke line around the logo

## 3. Color System

Balencia uses a vibrant, intentional color system built on the **60 / 30 / 10** ratio. Burnt Orange leads the brand. Forest Green signals growth and success. Royal Purple marks the coach's voice.

### Color Hierarchy

| Ratio | Role | Color | Hex | RGB | When to Use |
|-------|------|-------|-----|-----|-------------|
| **60%** | Primary | Burnt Orange | `#FF5E00` | 255, 94, 0 | CTAs, buttons, headlines, hero accents, user's voice, streaks, primary highlights |
| **30%** | Secondary | Forest Green | `#34A853` | 52, 168, 83 | Growth, success, completion, recovery, milestones, positive deltas, reward states |
| **10%** | Accent | Royal Purple | `#7F24FF` | 127, 36, 255 | Coach voice, AI insights, premium moments, transformation, projected data |

### Color Usage Rules

**Burnt Orange — use for:**
- Primary buttons and CTAs
- Main hero accents
- Active user paths
- The user's line on charts
- Streaks and effort states
- Brand mark on dark backgrounds

**Burnt Orange — do not use for:**
- Every background (it's the hero, not the wallpaper)
- Every icon at once
- Large flat areas combined with full-strength green and purple

**Forest Green — use for:**
- Positive deltas and success confirmations
- Recovery states and goal completion
- Milestone dots and progress arrival
- "Done" actions and reward states
- Growth-themed marketing content

**Forest Green — do not use for:**
- Primary purchase/action buttons (unless the action is completion or success)
- Alerting users to stress
- Asking for input

**Royal Purple — use for:**
- AI coach moments and insight chips
- Projected lines on charts
- Premium states and transformation moments
- Sleep, reflection, and intelligence moments
- Small accents

**Royal Purple — do not use for:**
- Large wallpaper-like backgrounds
- Every AI feature simultaneously
- Primary CTA buttons

### The 60 / 30 / 10 Rule

Every Balencia composition follows this ratio:

- **60% Burnt Orange** — the primary visual driver and action system
- **30% Forest Green** — growth, completion, recovery, and positive feedback
- **10% Royal Purple** — coach voice, AI, premium insight, and transformation

**Critical rule**: Use **one hero color per surface**. Never place orange, green, and purple all at full saturation in one composition. The only place where all three may meet is in the journey gradient.

### Neutral Palette — Warm-Cool Ink

Balencia uses a warm-cool neutral system with dark ink tones and warm paper tones. The system is dark-first — ink backgrounds are the primary canvas.

**Light Neutrals (Paper)**

| `100` | Hover backgrounds, light accents |

### Gradient System — The Journey Palette

| Token | Purpose | Visual |
|-------|---------|--------|
| `--grad-progress` | Progress movement | Teal/green into warm progress color |

**Gradient rules:**
- Gradients represent movement, journey, and status
- Use in charts, hero glows, and progress lines
- Do not use unrelated gradients
- Do not use heavy dark gradients in graphs
- Do not use a three-color gradient unless it is the approved journey palette

| Orange + Green (journey gradient) | Progress charts, milestone content | Growth and energy |
| Green + Ink-900 | Success/completion on dark | Calm achievement |
| Green + Paper tones | Growth content on light surfaces | Fresh, positive |
| Purple + Ink-900 | Coach/AI moments | Intelligent, premium |
| White + Ink-900 | Body text on dark, dark sections | Sophisticated, readable |
| Ink-700 + Paper-100 | Body text on light | Warm, grounded |

### Forbidden Color Pairings

| Combination | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Purple text on Orange background | Insufficient contrast, visual vibration |
| Orange text on Purple background | Same problem — never layer primary on accent |
| All three brand colors at full saturation in one composition | Visual chaos — use one hero color per surface |
| Pure black (`#000000`) as background | Use ink-900 `#0A0A0F` or ink tones instead |
| Non-brand colors (random blues, reds, teals) | Dilutes the palette — stay within the system |

## 4. Typography System

### Typography Principle

> Sora carries the whole system — display, body, and accent.

Sora is a single geometric sans typeface used at extremes of weight and size to create hierarchy. The italic light at the script accent gives a single emotional word its breath.

### Primary Typeface: Sora

**Font Family Declaration**:
```css
font-family: "Sora", system-ui, sans-serif;
```

**Tailwind Configuration**:
```js
module.exports = {
  theme: {
    extend: {
      fontFamily: {
        sans: ["Sora", "system-ui", "sans-serif"],
      },
    },
  },
}
```

### Logo Font: Chillax

Chillax is used exclusively for the logo and wordmark. Never in UI, body, or headings.

| Use | Typeface | Weight | Notes |
|-----|----------|--------|-------|
| Logo display | Chillax | 800 (ExtraBold) | Bold, geometric, rounded |
| Logo accent | Chillax | 700 (Bold) | Wordmark style |

### Type Scale

| Token | Size / Line-Height | Weight | Letter Spacing | Usage |
|-------|-------------------|--------|----------------|-------|
| `display-xl` | 96 / 92 | Black (900) | -0.025em | Hero / billboard. One per page max. |
| `display-l` | 64 / 60 | Bold (700) | -0.02em | Section titles |
| `h1` | 48 / 52 | Bold (700) | -0.015em | Page headings |
| `h2` | 36 / 39 | Bold (700) | default / tight | Section headings |
| `h3` | 28 / 34 | Semibold (600) | default / tight | Card titles |
| `lead` | 20 / 29 | Regular (400) | default | Intro paragraphs |
| `body` | 16 / 25 | Regular (400) | default | **Default body text** |
| `eyebrow` | 12 | Semibold (600) | +0.12em | Section labels (UPPERCASE) |

### Accent Typography

Use **Sora 700 Bold** for one emotional word in a headline. Maximum **two emotional words per page**.

Examples: *balance.* | *progress.* | *clarity.* | *attention.*

The accent word or the trailing brand dot is the only element that should receive brand color in a heading. Build hierarchy through size and weight, not by coloring every heading.

### Font Weights

| Weight | Value | When to Use |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Regular | 400 | Body text, lead paragraphs, secondary text |
| Semibold | 600 | Card titles (H3), eyebrow labels, buttons, interactive elements |
| Bold | 700 | Headings (H1-H2), display titles, accent words |
| Black | 900 | Display XL hero headlines only |

### Typography Rules

1. **Sentence case everywhere.** Buttons, titles, navigation, cards, form labels, tabs. Never Title Case in UI. The wordmark is the exception because it is a logo, not typed UI text.
2. **No exclamation marks.** Energy comes from rhythm, layout, and the brand period — not punctuation.
3. **The brand period is sacred.** Use the period intentionally in headlines, wordmark, and brand moments. Do not replace it with an exclamation mark.
4. **Hierarchy by size and weight.** Build visual hierarchy through extreme differences in size and weight, not through color. Only the accent word or trailing brand dot gets brand color.
5. **Minimum body text**: 16px on web, 14px on mobile.
6. **Maximum line length**: 75 characters per line for body text. Use `max-width: 65ch` as default.
7. **Paragraph spacing**: Equal to the line-height (e.g., 16px body at 25px line-height = 25px margin-bottom).
8. **Numbers**: Use `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums` for data, metrics, and any numeric display.

## 5. Brand Elements & Visual Language

### 5.1 Continuous Stroke Line System

The line system represents voice, journey, breath, progress, and the living state of the product. It should feel organic, continuous, and in motion.

**Line Types**

| Line Type | Meaning | Visual |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **Past / Projected** | History and prediction | Continuous stroke with loop-like tension |
| **Breathing Line** | Idle / voice mode | Curved organic line for coach or listening state |
| **Path of Progress** | Orange to green | Progress line moving from user effort into arrival/recovery |
| **Hero / Billboard** | Brand expression | 12px stroke on brand color, used at large scale |

**Stroke Widths**

| Token | Width | Role |
|-------|-------|------|
| `--stroke-thin` | 2px | Thin detail, subtle UI |
| `--stroke-base` | 4px | Base weight, standard use |
| `--stroke-bold` | 8px | Bold emphasis |
| `--stroke-poster` | 12px | Poster / billboard / hero |

**Color Meanings**

| Color | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Orange | The user's voice |
| Green | Progress and arrival |
| Purple | The coach and AI |
| Gradient | Graphs and stats |

**Line Opacity by Background**

| Background | Line Color | Opacity |
|------------|------------|---------|
| Burnt Orange | White | 60-80% |
| Royal Purple | White | 60-80% |
| Ink-900 / dark | White or Orange | 40-60% |
| White / Paper tones | Burnt Orange | 20-40% |
| Photography | White | 50-70% |

**Where to use:**
- Hero sections (behind or around headline)
- Section dividers and transitions
- Background textures on branded sections
- Social media templates
- App onboarding and achievement screens
- Data visualization (connecting to chart language)
- Coach/listening state animations

**Where NOT to use:**
- On data-heavy screens or tables (distracts from content)
- At small sizes where curves become illegible
- As borders or containers (they are decorative, not structural)
- Overlapping with body text (only behind or beside headlines)
- As input field borders
- Wrapped around the logo

**Line System Rules**

Do:
- Use round caps and round joins, always
- Keep each line continuous — one unbroken stroke
- Animate the line so it draws itself (stroke drawing, not fade-in)
- Use one line motif per surface, maximum

Do not:
- Break the line into disconnected fragments
- Fade the line in — it should draw itself
- Center it symmetrically
- Use it as an input border
- Wrap it around the logo
- Use more than one line motif per surface

**Motion Behavior**

The line should feel like a journey. When animated, use stroke drawing instead of opacity fades.

```css
.brand-line {
  stroke-linecap: round;
  stroke-linejoin: round;
  stroke-dasharray: 1;
  stroke-dashoffset: 1;
  animation: draw-line var(--dur-flow) var(--ease-flow) forwards;
}

@keyframes draw-line {
  to {
    stroke-dashoffset: 0;
  }
}
```

| Token | Duration | Use |
|-------|----------|-----|

| `--dur-base` | 280ms | UI default transitions |
| `--dur-slow` | 520ms | Panels, surface transitions |
| `--dur-flow` | 1.2s | Line drawing and signature motion |

**Motion Rules:**
- Use motion to clarify state, not decorate everything
- Avoid linear easing
- Avoid sudden jumps
- Use longer motion (`--dur-flow`) for continuous line drawing
- Use shorter motion (`--dur-fast`) for UI feedback
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` settings in implementation

## 6. Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice (Consistent)

Balencia's voice is that of a **Grounded Coach** — curious, warm, and quietly confident. Not a corporate entity, not a casual buddy. A coach who questions before it answers, listens before it suggests.

| Trait | Sound Like | Don't Sound Like |
|-------|------------|-----------------|
| **Grounded** | "Your data shows a clear pattern." | "We think maybe possibly..." |
| **Curious** | "What's worth your attention today?" | "Here are your 12 tasks for today." |
| **Warm** | "Let's work on this together." | "Proceed to the next module." |
| **Playful** | "21 days. That's a habit now." | "OMG YOU'RE CRUSHING IT!!!" |
| **Quietly Confident** | "You missed 3 sessions. Let's reset." | "We noticed some variability in your engagement metrics." |

### Signature Phrase

> **"What's worth your attention today?"**

This is the best example of Balencia's tone. It is curious, calm, and useful. It does not push or shame the user.

### Tone (Adaptive by Context)

Tone shifts based on context. Voice stays constant.

**Celebrating success** — genuinely warm, grounded:
- "5 days hitting your protein goal. You found your rhythm."
- "Best sleep score this month. Whatever you did last night, do it again."
- "21 days straight. That's not a streak, that's a habit."

**Supporting through difficulty** — gentle, honest, no judgment:
- "Tough day. One rest day never undid anyone's progress."
- "Energy's low today — I can see it in your data. That's okay."
- "Stress is real. Let's focus on one small thing you can control right now."

**Delivering insights** — curious, helpful, specific:
- "Your workouts are 40% better after 7+ hours of sleep."
- "You tend to skip meals on meeting-heavy days. Want to plan ahead?"
- "Your mood and morning routine are connected. Consistency matters for you."

**Nudging** — friendly suggestion, easy to dismiss:
- "15-minute walk? Your energy usually dips around now."
- "Quick check-in? 30 seconds. Or skip if you're not feeling it."
- "You usually feel better after journaling. Want to get some thoughts out?"

### SIA — The AI Coach

SIA is Balencia's AI coach personality. SIA is not a chatbot. SIA is a grounded guide who knows you, adapts to you, and speaks with the voice and tone defined above.

**SIA's traits:**
- Remembers your patterns and preferences
- Connects dots across wellness, productivity, and personal growth
- Asks good questions — prompts reflection, doesn't just inform
- Celebrates wins with genuine warmth (not corporate excitement)
- Never judges, always encourages effort over perfection
- Gives easy outs — "no pressure", "or skip if you're not feeling it"

### Writing Guidelines

**Do:**
- Use sentence case everywhere (buttons, titles, navigation, cards)
- Be concise — say more with less
- Be specific — use data and specifics, not vague encouragement
- Write like you're speaking with a trusted friend
- Use "you" — direct address creates connection
- Use numbers — quantify when possible
- Give easy outs — reduce commitment anxiety
- Use active voice
- Let the brand period carry energy

**Don't:**
- No Title Case in UI (wordmark is the only exception)
- No exclamation marks — energy comes from rhythm and the brand period
- No jargon — avoid medical or technical terms without explanation
- No preaching — no lecturing, no guilt-tripping, ever
- No corporate speak — "leverage," "synergy," "optimize your wellness journey"
- No fake positivity — authentic beats cheerful
- No emoji overload — max 1 per message in-app, zero in marketing/web copy
- No demanding — suggest, don't command
- No "crush your goals" style language

**Word choice:**

| Never Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|------------|
| Optimize your wellness journey | Feel better every day |
| Leverage your data insights | Here's what your data shows |
| Achieve optimal performance | Perform at your best |
| Your metrics indicate | Your data shows |
| Suboptimal sleep detected | Your sleep was off last night |
| Unlock your potential | Discover what works for you |
| Holistic approach | The full picture |
| Crush your goals | Find your rhythm |
| Maximum performance unlocked | You're ready for a steady session |

### Voice by Touchpoint

| Touchpoint | Tone Lean | Notes |
|------------|-----------|-------|
| Website / marketing | More grounded, aspirational | Sell the transformation, not features |
| App conversation (SIA) | More warm, curious | Feels like a 1:1 coaching session |
| Social media | Grounded + warm, punchy | Short, specific insights. Not motivational quotes. |
| Email | Direct, respectful of time | Get to the point. CTA in first 3 lines. |
| Push notifications | Ultra-concise, encouraging | One line. No fluff. Easy to dismiss. |

## 8. Do's and Don'ts

### Color

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Lead with Burnt Orange — it's the primary visual driver (60%) | Use purple as the dominant color |
| Use Forest Green for growth, success, and completion (30%) | Use all three brand colors at full saturation in one composition |
| Use Royal Purple sparingly for coach/AI moments (10%) | Introduce random non-brand colors |
| Use one hero color per surface | Use pure black (`#000000`) — use ink tones instead |
| Use ink tones for dark surfaces, paper tones for light | Mix old neutrals with the new ink/paper system |

### Typography

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use Sora for everything except the logo | Use decorative, cursive, or novelty fonts |
| Use Chillax only for the logo wordmark | Use Chillax in UI, headings, or body text |
| Build hierarchy with weight (Black/Bold/Semibold) and size | Build hierarchy with color alone |
| Use sentence case everywhere | Use Title Case in UI (wordmark is the exception) |
| Maintain 16px minimum body text | Use body text smaller than 14px in any context |
| Use the brand period intentionally | Use exclamation marks — the period carries the energy |

### Layout & Composition

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Build dark, warm, premium layouts (dark-first) | Build generic SaaS templates with no personality |
| Use generous whitespace and capsule-friendly radii | Crowd elements with tight spacing |
| Use one continuous stroke line per surface | Fill backgrounds with tangled spaghetti-like lines |
| Use warm orange gradient overlays on photos | Use black overlays on photos |
| Show warm, natural photography of real people | Use cold blue-tinted stock photos or AI-generated images |
| Mask photography into the rounded shape system | Use unmasked rectangular photos that break the visual system |

### Lines

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use round caps and round joins, always | Break the line into disconnected fragments |
| Keep each line as one continuous stroke | Fade the line in — it draws itself |
| Animate the line with stroke drawing | Center the line symmetrically |
| Use one line motif per surface maximum | Use the line as input field borders |
| Match line color to meaning (orange=user, green=progress, purple=coach) | Wrap lines around the logo |

### Data Visualization

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use solid orange for past / user data | Use three-color gradients |
| Use dashed purple for projections | Use dark color gradients in charts |
| Use green dots for milestones | Add unrelated color codes |
| Use approved gradients for stats | Make decorative charts with no real data |

### Photography

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use real people with natural light | Use AI-generated stock photography |
| Use slight grain and warm color grade | Use overly polished wellness-influencer imagery |
| Mask images into the shape system | Use cold clinical imagery |
| Use the background/skin-tone pairings | Use generic gym photography |

### The Anti-Pattern (What NOT to Build)

A generic, cold design with:
- No Burnt Orange presence — the brand is invisible
- No continuous stroke line motif — no visual signature
- No warmth or personality — could belong to any tech company
- Template-driven layout with no brand-specific decisions

**Every new deliverable should be checked against this anti-pattern.** If it could be mistaken for a generic SaaS site with no brand personality, it fails the guidelines.
