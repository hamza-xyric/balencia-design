Section 1

Core Philosophy & Identity

1.1 What is the single sentence that describes what Balencia IS?
Aligned
Hamza

Balencia is your clarity and your guide. For anyone struggling with discipline, productivity, structure, or needing motivation. Core principle: "Balencia sees what we can't see. Balencia connects different parts of your life system together."

Salman

"Balencia is your AI life coach that connects your fitness, finances, faith, career, and relationships into one system — and shows you the patterns you'd never spot alone."

Merged
"Balencia is your AI life coach that sees what you can't — connecting every part of your life into one system and guiding you to be better in all of it."
1.2 "Balencia sees what we can't see" — what does this mean concretely in the UI?
Aligned
Merged

Both agree on a multi-layered approach:

  1. SIA's conversational insights — Proactive cross-domain observations in natural language
  2. Visual intelligence layer — Knowledge Graph, Life Correlation Matrix, domain-colored connections
  3. Interactive drill-down — Clicking a domain shows its correlations with others
  4. Layered delivery — Daily via SIA, weekly via digest, on-demand via graph/insights

Hamza adds: Life Correlation system is Balencia's commercial differentiator and marketing tool — proprietary data positioning.

Salman adds: The "holy shit" moment — first time SIA tells you something true about your life you didn't know.

1.3 What is the emotional arc of a user's first 5 minutes?
Review
Hamza

Recognition / Relatability (0-60s) → Motivation / Hope (1-3 min) → Excitement / Anticipation (3-5 min)

  • TikTok-style relatability — "what I'm thinking in my heart, he's saying it"
  • Islamic motivational content as inspiration for the fire Balencia should ignite
  • 30-second video to create a "wow" moment
Salman

"This feels different" (0-15s) → "It's talking to me" (15-45s) → "It gets me" (45s-2min) → "It's already working" (2-3:30) → "I want to come back" (3:30-5:00)

  • More screen-by-screen UX flow detail
  • Key principle: never show an empty state — app feels alive from minute 1
To resolve: Emotional goals are aligned (relate → motivate → excite). The difference is execution — Hamza wants heavier video/content opening, Salman wants a faster UX-driven flow. Connects to question 3.4 (intro video).

Review
Hamza

Primarily chatbot-driven with visual enhancements — SIA drives conversation, enriched with visual guidance, recommendation chips, and popup cards.

Salman

3.3 What information do you absolutely need before the user can start?
Contradicting
Hamza — Required upfront
  1. Name
  2. Life areas of interest
  3. Age/gender
  4. Integrations (WHOOP, Calendar, Spotify)
  5. Subscription plan

Salman — Required upfront
  1. Name
  2. Life areas of interest (1-3 domains)

Deferred: Motivation level, age/gender, integrations, subscription

Asking for subscription before proving value is risky. But connecting integrations early means richer data sooner.

To resolve: Duration (30s vs 5-10s) and format (pre-recorded video vs motion graphics). Core question: does the "wow" come from a preview or from the live AI experience?

6.2 The AI-first pattern — does it apply equally to ALL domains?
Contradicting
Hamza

Yes, equally across ALL domains. Same pattern: AI suggests → user reviews → accepts/edits/skips → AI learns. No domain exempt. SIA is the coach across the board.

Salman

  • AI-heavy (80%): Workouts, meals, schedule, learning

Why this matters: Spirituality and relationships are deeply personal. "You should pray this way" is very different from "Here's your workout." Salman's graduated approach respects domain sensitivity. Hamza's uniform approach keeps things consistent.

Why this matters: Backend features existing ≠ equal UI/UX effort. Redesigning 9 full domain UIs simultaneously is massive. A phased approach could ship the 3 core domains beautifully, then roll out the rest. Prioritizing means highest-impact domains get the most design attention first.

Section 10

Gamification & Motivation

10.1 How prominent are streaks?
Review
Hamza

B) Present but not central. Visible, celebrated at milestones, but not THE motivation. "The value proposition is insight, not streaks."

10.2 Achievement system — how does it work?
Contradicting
Hamza

Full RPG gamification system (WoW/Habitica-inspired):

  • Goals become missions/quests, sub-goals become sub-quests
  • Users level up with XP, each domain is a skill tree
  • Party system, equipment/rewards, narrative framing
  • Meaningful consequences (HP loss for missed tasks)
  • Must feel premium/mature, not cartoonish
Salman

Standard achievement system:

  • Cross-domain: "Life Connector", "Life Balanced"
  • Domain-specific: "30-day prayer streak", "Saved $500"
  • Cross-domain achievements celebrated more (unique to Balencia)
  • No RPG framework
This is the biggest design direction divergence. The RPG system affects every screen, every interaction, every celebration. Hamza raised the critical tension himself: "How do you make RPG gamification engaging without it feeling childish, gimmicky, or visually ugly?" This must be resolved before any gamification design work begins.

Merged

10.4: Yes. Low = heavy gamification. Medium = balanced. High = lighter, data-focused.
