# 05 — Visual Identity

The design language, captured verbatim from source (`materials.ts` for the palette; `MASTER-CONTEXT.md` for atmosphere, typography, and guardrails).

## Brand color weighting

The palette is governed by a **60 / 30 / 10** rule:

| Weight | Role | Color | Hex | Used for |
|--------|------|-------|-----|----------|
| **60%** | Primary | Burnt Orange | `#FF5E00` | Energy/connection accents, SIA glow, primary CTAs |
| **30%** | Secondary | Forest Green | `#34A853` | Success, achievement, "nature" domains |
| **10%** | Tertiary | Royal Purple | `#7F24FF` | AI / intelligence / coach cues |

## Atmosphere & mood

The experience is **dark, premium, and night-time** throughout. Sky/atmosphere direction:

- **Zenith:** ink-blue `#0A0A0F` with scattered stars.
- **Horizon:** warm amber gradient blending upward into purple-indigo.
- **Aurora:** subtle orange-purple bands drifting slowly across the upper sky.
- **Fog/depth:** warm amber-tinted atmospheric haze, denser with distance.

The net feel: **warm darkness** — not cold cyberpunk. Orange/amber warmth glowing out of a deep blue-black field.

## Aesthetic guardrails

The design was steered by an explicit "draw from / avoid" table — useful as art-direction for the website too:

| Draw from | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|
| Blade Runner 2049 warmth (amber fog, volumetric light shafts) | Lego / toy-like proportions |
| Apple spatial computing (clean geometry, soft glass) | Neon overload or generic cyberpunk |
| UE5 architectural visualization (precise detail, cinematic framing) | Photorealistic simulation |
| Dark sophistication, night-time grandeur | Daytime scenes, suburban scale; loud/garish neon |
